Racial Disparities

Recognize the racial disparities to understand the urgency and importance of the Black Lives Matter Movement

Police Killings

Black people have been disproportionately killed by the US police for decades. The issue of racial bias in police shootings are rooted in a history of discrimination against the Black community. The origins of this racially biased police system can be traced back to the “Slave Patrol.”

Unemployment

The first economic victims of the Covid-19 pandemic were the disproportionately employed numbers of Black and Brown service workers. As a result, it has also emphasized the economic inequities between Black workers and White workers that have persisted for years. This magnified devastation for Black people stem from a long history of racial exclusion, discrimination, and inequality.

COVID-19

Communities of color are disproportionately affected by COVID-19 across the United States, with their death rates tripled that of the white community. People of color bear the most burden from the virus not by bad luck but rather by an ongoing and persistent systemic issue. The disparities in healthcare for the Black communities has been an issue in America for a long time now. It is important to see from the COVID-19 data that these health disparities are still very apparent and should be realized.
